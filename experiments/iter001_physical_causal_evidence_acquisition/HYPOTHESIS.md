# Iteration 001: Prospective Physical Causal Evidence Acquisition

Status: PRE-REGISTERED

Frozen: 2026-07-14

Mission: Active Causal Mission Assurance

## Decision question

Can a prospective campaign acquire and admit a pilot corpus of at least 30 complete physical
incident dossiers that is sufficient to test open-world mechanism diagnosis, safe discriminating
actions, independently assessed recovery, and grouped transfer without substituting labels,
windows, simulator branches, task reward, or model judgment for physical evidence?

This iteration qualifies the evidence construct. It does not evaluate a learned model and cannot
establish diagnosis, intervention, recovery, safety, transfer, product, or economic value.

## Relation to iteration 000

Iteration 000 returned `BLOCKED_EVIDENCE`. NASA ADAPT passed source integrity, parser integrity,
and truth separation, but contributed only 16 incidents from one hardware identity and no
independently reviewed ambiguity sets or safe discriminating actions. Its proof and consumed
verification authority are immutable inputs to this iteration and must not be rerun or rewritten.

The minimum count remains 30, but it is only a pilot admission floor. It is not a power calculation
and cannot support a population or state-of-the-art claim by itself.

## Prior exposure disclosure

The decision is not blind to the public-data landscape. Before freezing this protocol, source
reconnaissance established the following:

- no screened public aerospace or robotics corpus jointly exposes independent physical mechanism
  adjudication, multiple pre-outcome hypotheses, an independently reviewed safe diagnostic action,
  commanded and realized intervention traces, an executed recovery, independent settled outcome
  authority, explicit multimodal clocks, and hardware-group transfer;
- NASA NOS3 can generate controlled simulator branches but has no prebuilt qualifying physical
  incident corpus, carries an unresolved top-level license identifier inconsistency, and includes
  many separately licensed submodules;
- Basilisk is a permissively licensed spacecraft simulator candidate, but simulator evidence cannot
  carry the physical or product claim;
- ALFA contains 47 real fixed-wing flights with fault and emergency-action evidence, but one
  airframe and observational treatment assignment cannot supply paired counterfactuals or hardware
  transfer;
- ESA-AD v2 contains real multi-mission spacecraft telemetry and commands, but anonymization and
  absent action-outcome adjudication limit it to shift, calibration, and shortcut controls;
- NASA DXC provides injected mechanisms and recommended responses, but its published protocol says
  recovery actions are not executed;
- the Petrobras 3W corpus provides real multichannel well events, expert labels, identities, and
  permissive data rights, but no prospectively reviewed ambiguity set or controlled diagnostic and
  recovery outcome;
- RoboMIND, SAFE, RoboFAC, RoboFail, Robometer, Guardian, and AHA provide useful failure,
  intervention-time, correction, reward-proxy, or synthetic controls, but none satisfies the full
  incident contract;
- current safe active model-discrimination work provides a method baseline, not a qualifying public
  corpus; and
- existing recovery work can improve task success while still omitting physical mechanism
  adjudication and outcome independence.

These findings kill a public-dataset-only strategy before download or training. Public sources may
enter only under the evidentiary roles frozen below. They cannot be pooled to manufacture a complete
dossier.

## Scientific unit

The unit is one `root_incident_group`, defined as one independently initiated physical departure
from nominal operation on one identified system, followed from the frozen evidence cutoff through
mechanism adjudication, diagnostic action, recovery attempt, settling horizon, and recurrence check.

Telemetry rows, frames, windows, crops, question-answer pairs, simulator branches, repeated model
calls, annotations, and repeated measurements of one episode are never additional incidents.
Repeated fault injections from one test session share an independence group unless the system is
returned to a preregistered baseline, a new initiation is independently executed, and the grouping
rule was frozen before outcome access. All estimates and future splits cluster at the root incident
and acquisition-session levels.

## Evidence tracks

Three tracks remain separate in every report and artifact.

### Physical admission track

Only this track counts toward the 30-incident floor. It uses prospective, human-approved testbed
execution or existing real-world evidence that already contains every required field and permits
independent audit. The target portfolio includes at least one aerospace or spacecraft-like family
and at least one robotic family.

### Causal laboratory track

Pinned simulators may validate snapshot restoration, paired no-op and intervention branches,
clocking, causal labels, blocked unsafe actions, and settled-state oracles. NOS3 is conditional on a
complete dependency and rights disposition. Basilisk is the current permissive fallback. Simulator
branches never count as physical incidents and are always reported separately.

### Independent reality and control track

ALFA, ESA-AD, 3W, NASA DXC, NASA ADAPT, and selected robotics corpora may provide real-distribution
anchors, parsers, priors, baselines, and shortcut controls under exact source locks. A missing field
cannot be borrowed from another dataset. Recommendations and telecommands are observational unless
their execution and outcome are independently established.

## Complete incident contract

Every counted physical dossier must pass every gate below on the same root incident. Separate
counts per gate may be reported diagnostically but cannot be intersected by assumption.

1. Source and rights integrity. The source authority, immutable version or retrieval time, byte
   count, content hash, governing terms, redistribution status, derivative-data permission,
   commercial-use status, retention or deletion duties, and independent-review access route are
   recorded before use. Unknown or conflicting terms block admission.
2. Physical identity. The system family, exact hardware identity, relevant configuration, site,
   acquisition session, and root incident group are explicit. Simulated, hand-drawn, and generated
   cases are marked and excluded from the physical count.
3. Pre-outcome evidence. At least two operational evidence modalities are present before the frozen
   cutoff. Filename, directory, task, site, system identity, annotation, and truth-derived metadata
   are excluded from model-visible evidence.
4. Clock contract. Each modality declares clock name, unit, epoch or monotonic origin, sample rule,
   synchronization transform, measured or bounded alignment error, and missingness. Commanded and
   realized action times are distinct. Ambiguous or unbounded alignment blocks admission.
5. Open-world ambiguity. Before truth or outcome access, a content-addressed hypothesis set records
   at least two plausible known physical mechanisms and exactly one unknown mechanism with
   normalized prior mass. The cheapest deterministic evidence-only rule must not already identify
   the mechanism without the proposed diagnostic action.
6. Mechanism authority. A separately sealed truth record identifies the physical mechanism through
   controlled injection, teardown, nondestructive evaluation, calibrated reference measurement, or
   another prospectively accepted physical method. A task label, model explanation, reward, or
   operator guess is insufficient.
7. Safe diagnostic plan. Before execution, a distinct safety reviewer approves the allowed action,
   constraints, aborts, risk bound, predicted outcomes under every known hypothesis, cost, duration,
   and approval receipt. The action must be capable of changing the posterior in its frozen outcome
   model and have strictly positive expected information gain; approval alone does not assert that
   it succeeds.
8. Diagnostic execution. The selected action is actually attempted by an authorized executor.
   Command, acknowledgment, realized actuation, constraint margins, aborts, observations, duration,
   direct cost, and realized adverse events are recorded. A recommendation or command log without
   realized-state evidence fails.
9. Recovery execution. A content-addressed recovery plan is frozen after diagnostic evidence and
   before recovery outcome access, approved for the testbed, and actually attempted. Failed
   recoveries remain admitted when all evidence is complete; no-op or undocumented manual reset is
   not a recovery attempt.
10. Settled outcome. A verifier independent of the hypothesis proposer, action selector, recovery
    proposer, and executor assesses action validity, target validity, physical settling, constraint
    satisfaction, and recurrence over a testbed-specific horizon frozen before execution. Task
    reward, VLM judgment, and proposer self-assessment cannot serve as sole authority.
11. Role independence. Named assignments, independence groups, and content-addressed conflict
    disclosures are recorded for source stewardship, evidence curation, mechanism adjudication,
    hypothesis proposal, safety review, action selection, execution, recovery proposal, outcome
    verification, and statistical analysis. The forbidden role combinations above fail closed.
    Different display names inside one independence group do not establish independence.
12. Transfer and cost fields. System family, hardware identity, fault family, environment, site,
    session, and acquisition lineage are available for grouped splits. Engineering time, test time,
    downtime, compute, action cost, and realized risk are recorded as separate resource planes;
    unknown values remain unknown rather than zero.

## Frozen corpus coverage gates

All requirements are conjunctive:

- at least 30 complete physical root incident groups;
- at least three physical system families;
- at least two hardware identities per system family and at least six identities overall;
- at least three fault families;
- at least six complete incidents from each included system family;
- at least six complete incidents from each included fault family;
- at least three complete incidents from each included hardware identity;
- no system family or fault family contributes more than 50 percent of eligible incidents;
- every included system family covers at least two fault families;
- every included fault family occurs on at least two hardware identities;
- every claim-bearing fault family occurs in at least two system families;
- at least one aerospace or spacecraft-like family and at least one robotic family;
- no root incident, acquisition session, hardware identity, or truth-derived lineage crosses a
  future train, calibration, and test boundary; and
- 100 percent of counted dossiers pass the complete incident contract.

Three system families are required because two families permit one transfer contrast but no
replicated leave-one-family assessment. These are admission floors, not evidence that the eventual
performance experiment is adequately powered. Power and sample-size calculations occur after
admission from blinded pilot variance components and before model development.

## Frozen controls

The admission implementation must include executable positive and negative fixtures for every gate.
At minimum it must reject:

- a corpus whose separate per-gate counts exceed 30 but whose complete intersection does not;
- windows, branches, or repeated annotations presented as independent incidents;
- simulated evidence presented as physical evidence;
- missing, mutable, conflicting, noncommercial, or unreviewable rights presented as cleared;
- filename, path, task, system, site, timestamp, fault label, or annotation leakage into evidence;
- a one-modality case, duplicated modality, stationary-image proxy, shuffled modality, or
  unbounded clock transform presented as multimodal evidence;
- a truth record opened before hypothesis and diagnostic-plan commitments;
- a known-only hypothesis set with no unknown mass;
- self-review, prohibited role overlap, forged approval, or outcome verification by the proposer;
- a diagnostic recommendation without commanded and realized execution evidence;
- a recovery command, manual reset, task reward, confidence score, or model judgment presented as a
  settled physical recovery;
- source, task, system identity, randomly initialized identity embedding, and cheapest
  deterministic rules presented as causal multimodal performance; and
- no-op, random-safe, cheapest-safe, and wrong-but-safe actions omitted from later intervention
  comparisons.

The same validator path used for admission must execute these controls. Paper-only review is not a
gate.

## Split and outcome discipline

No model feature extraction, tuning, learned embedding, or outcome-driven exclusion occurs before
the incident registry and per-axis split locks are frozen. Hardware-family, hardware-identity, and
fault-family transfer use separate locks; a joint connected-component union cannot be used to erase
a properly crossed design. Future evaluation uses grouped leave-one-identity,
leave-one-system-family, and leave-one-fault-family assessments where the coverage graph permits
them. Results are clustered by root incident, session, and identity.

A later transfer claim requires a separately preregistered clustered power calculation and, even if
that calculation suggests fewer units, at least four system families, eight hardware identities,
four fault families, and two untouched claim-bearing family units. Passing this iteration does not
meet that claim threshold.

Admission itself is an exact census decision, not a sampled estimate: every counted dossier must
pass. Any later performance or value result must report an identity-clustered uncertainty interval.
The eventual primary value claim remains a positive 95 percent lower confidence bound on physical
benefit after diagnostic cost, delay, compute cost, false actions, and realized intervention risk,
with a separately preregistered safety non-inferiority gate.

## Resource and authority ceiling

The contract and admission-tooling phase authorizes local CPU execution, public metadata access,
source-document review, schema generation, and synthetic fixtures only. One admission audit may use
at most four CPU-hours, two elapsed hours, 16 GiB peak memory, 1 GiB downloaded metadata, and 2 GiB
peak staged bytes. Its ceiling is zero GPU hours, zero cloud jobs, zero paid provider calls, zero
live robot or flight authority, zero physical actuation, zero provider cost, and zero
redistribution of third-party data. Missing resource measurements invalidate the audit.

Every source download beyond metadata and every simulator or testbed execution requires a separate
prospective resource lease. Every physical action additionally requires a run-specific safety
envelope, approval receipt, executor, abort authority, and externally reviewable test plan committed
before execution. This preregistration does not grant those authorities.

## Stop rules

Stop source work at the first cheapest failing hard gate. Do not download a large corpus when its
license, identity, action, outcome, or clock metadata already disqualifies its intended role.

Stop the iteration and preserve a full-weight result when:

- a rights conflict cannot be resolved in writing;
- the complete-dossier count or coverage graph cannot meet the frozen floor;
- the evidence cutoff cannot be proven to precede truth, diagnostic, and recovery outcomes;
- safe diagnostic or recovery execution cannot be independently approved;
- a deliberately broken or placebo dossier passes admission;
- the cheapest deterministic rule makes the intended cases non-ambiguous; or
- a source, simulator, or testbed exceeds its separate approved resource or safety authority.

No threshold may be lowered after evidence inspection. Corrections must be prospective, explicit,
and append-only.

## Verdict classes

- `PASS_PILOT`: every complete-dossier and coverage gate passes, the registry and split
  lock are frozen, and all negative controls fail as intended. This authorizes only a separately
  preregistered cheap deterministic baseline phase.
- `BLOCKED_ACQUISITION`: integrity controls pass, but qualifying physical evidence or coverage is
  incomplete. This authorizes additional prospective acquisition under a new resource and safety
  authority, not training.
- `BLOCKED_RIGHTS`: the scientific fields may be available, but acquisition, processing,
  retention, independent review, or publication rights are absent, ambiguous, or conflicting. This
  authorizes rights resolution or source replacement only.
- `KILL_PUBLIC_SUBSTRATE`: public sources cannot provide the complete construct. This preserves
  their declared control roles and directs work to prospective testbeds; it is already supported by
  the disclosed reconnaissance and is not a novel result of this iteration.
- `KILL_CONSTRUCT`: the cheapest deterministic evidence-only baseline resolves the purportedly
  ambiguous cases or the action/outcome contract cannot represent the physical problem. This stops
  learned-model work.
- `INVALID`: source integrity, truth separation, chronology, role independence, approval,
  executable controls, or proof reconstruction fails.
- `INFRASTRUCTURE_NULL`: an authorized acquisition cannot complete for an infrastructure reason
  without evidence about the scientific construct.

## Expected artifacts

- versioned source, rights, resource, role, and clock manifests;
- content-addressed physical incident dossiers and separate synthetic/control registries;
- pre-outcome evidence, hypothesis, diagnostic-plan, action-observation, recovery-plan, and
  settled-outcome commitments;
- exact completeness and coverage report over the conjunctive incident set;
- connected-component split feasibility report;
- executable positive, negative, leakage, chronology, role, and count-inflation controls;
- machine-readable verdict and one human-readable result generated from the same object;
- append-only research-memory events for decisions, sources, rejected candidates, manual work,
  costs, failures, corrections, and handoff state; and
- an independent aerospace and robotics protocol review recorded before any physical execution.

## Forbidden claims

Regardless of verdict, this iteration cannot claim model performance, causal discovery accuracy,
diagnostic benefit, safe autonomy, recovery success, cross-hardware transfer, flight readiness,
robot deployment readiness, state of the art, product readiness, customer value, market value, or
economic return. Architecture, code quality, public source scale, simulator success, and an admitted
pilot corpus are not substitutes for those results.
